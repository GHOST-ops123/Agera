To revolutionize agriculture through smart, accessible technology that empowers farmersespecially smallholderswith the tools they need to diagnose plant diseases early, get real-time treatment recommendations, and improve crop yields sustainably.

Our Vision

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our agricultural services, crop diagnostics, and delivery solutions.

Agera is a mobile-based agricultural technology (AgriTech) platform that empowers smallholder farmers by providing real-time AI crop disease diagnostics, a curated marketplace for agricultural inputs, expert recommendations, and efficient delivery services. It connects farmers directly with Agro-vets and suppliers, all in one seamless digital ecosystem.
Agera is designed for:

Smallholder farmers seeking affordable, expert tools to increase yield and manage crop health.

Agro-vets and suppliers wanting to reach more customers and manage inventory digitally.

Delivery personnel who support rural logistics through optimized, app-managed delivery routes.
Farmers use their smartphone cameras to scan crops via the Agera app. The AI, trained on regional pests and crop diseases, provides a diagnosis with up to 98% accuracy, followed by treatment recommendations and product suggestions from nearby suppliers.
Yes, farmers can access all essential services, including diagnostics, marketplace browsing, and delivery coordination, for free through a freemium model. Agro-vets can choose between multiple subscription tiers to access additional features such as analytics, marketing tools, and premium support.
The Agera marketplace includes pesticides, fertilizers, seeds, farm tools, and other agricultural inputs provided by verified Agro-vets and local suppliers.
Deliveries are managed through a network of local motorbike riders ("Piki Piki") and logistics partners. The app features order tracking, optimized routing, and performance metrics to ensure fast and reliable service, even in remote areas.
Agera only partners with certified Agro-vets and suppliers who comply with local agricultural regulations. The platform also includes user reviews, product ratings, and a regulatory filter system to enhance transparency and trust.
Yes, Agera offers offline capabilities and local language support to ensure accessibility for farmers in low-connectivity areas.
Agera launched in Kenya and is scaling across East Africa, with a focus on high-agriculture regions. Expansion plans include additional African markets over the next five years.
Agro-vets and delivery personnel can register directly through the app or the Agera website. Upon onboarding, they receive training and access to tools tailored to help them grow and manage their business digitally.
